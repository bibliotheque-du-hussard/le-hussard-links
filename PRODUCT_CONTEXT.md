# Product Context

## What We Do

La Bibliotheque du Hussard is a community catalog for books and references shared by Le Hussard through public YouTube descriptions.

The app turns scattered video-description links into a searchable reading shelf. Each listed item keeps a clear path to the Amazon link and the YouTube video where the recommendation appeared, so visitors can find a book quickly without losing the original context.

## Who It Serves

- Viewers who remember a book mentioned by Le Hussard and want to find the link again.
- Readers browsing the channel's literary references without opening many video descriptions one by one.
- Supporters who want to use the public Amazon links already shared by the channel.

## Core Promise

Find the books cited by Le Hussard faster, with enough source context to understand where each link came from.

## What Belongs Here

- Public Amazon book links found in Le Hussard's YouTube descriptions.
- The source video title and YouTube link for each catalog item.
- A small set of adjacent official or partner destinations, such as La Giberne, Pass Culture, the channel, and the Bons a tirer podcast.
- Clear transparency that the site is community-made and not an official Le Hussard property.

## What Does Not Belong Here

- A general bookstore or editorial store independent from the channel's public links.
- A replacement for Le Hussard's YouTube descriptions, channel, publishing work, or partner sites.
- Private, scraped, or inferred information that was not publicly shared.
- Non-book clutter that distracts from finding the reading references.

## Product Principles

- Books first: the catalog should make reading links easy to scan and search.
- Source-aware: each item should keep the video context available but visually secondary.
- Transparent: visitors should understand that the site is unofficial and may include affiliate links.
- Lightweight: the experience should feel like a focused reference shelf, not a social feed or content platform.
- Community useful: additions should help viewers recover, browse, or understand the channel's shared references.

## Current State

As of the included data file generated on 2026-05-30, the catalog contains 638 Amazon links from 204 Le Hussard videos.
